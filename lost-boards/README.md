# Early–Mid Boards (Lost Projects)

Between my first two surviving boards and my more recent designs, I created several additional PCBs that unfortunately did not survive a Windows corruption incident. These boards represented my transition from beginner‑level designs into more confident, functional hardware — but no files or screenshots remain.

Even though the physical data is gone, these boards played a major role in shaping my design skills and workflow.

---

##What These Boards Included

These lost boards covered a range of early experiments, including:
- Simple microcontroller breakout boards  
- Early sensor integration attempts  
- Power regulation circuits  
- Prototype control boards  
- Layout practice on 2‑layer designs  

They weren’t polished, but they were essential stepping stones.

---

## What I Learned

Even without the files, these boards taught me several important lessons:

### **1. Iteration is everything**
Each board pushed me a little further:
- Cleaner routing  
- Better placement  
- More reliable power paths  
- More confidence with footprints and libraries  

### **2. Backups matter**
Losing several boards at once forced me to:
- Switch to Linux  
- Adopt Git for version control  
- Keep cloud backups  
- Document projects more thoroughly  

### **3. Skill progression is nonlinear**
These boards were messy, experimental, and sometimes chaotic — but they built the foundation for the more advanced boards that came after.

### **4. Not every project needs to be preserved to be valuable**
The experience and intuition gained from these boards still influence how I design today.
