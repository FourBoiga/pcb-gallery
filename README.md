# PCB Gallery

This repository is a curated collection of my PCB design work, organized chronologically from my earliest surviving boards to more recent projects. Each board represents a step in my progression as a hardware designer — from simple early layouts to mechanically integrated rocket systems and functional avionics.

Click any project below to view its dedicated folder, documentation, images, and videos.

---

## Project Index (Oldest → Newest)

### 1. First PCB — Early Prototype  
Folder: [`board1_first-pcb`](./board1_first-pcb)  

My earliest surviving PCB. The schematic was completed, but the layout was still in progress. This board marks the beginning of my PCB design journey and shows my first attempts at structured schematic capture and routing.

---

### 2. Gimbaling Rocket Engine PCB — Fusion 360 Model  
Folder: [`board2_gimbal-rocket-pcb`](./board2_gimbal-rocket-pcb)  

A mechanically integrated PCB designed for a model rocket with a gimbaling engine. Although the electrical files were lost, the full Fusion 360 model survived and includes detailed mechanical fit-checking and component placement.

---

### 3. Rocket Club Airbrakes Controller — 2-Layer PCB  
Folder: [`board3_rocket-club-pcb`](./board3_rocket-club-pcb)  

A 2-layer board built around an Arduino Nano, barometer, and servo outputs. Designed to fit the internal diameter of an ~80 mm rocket airframe tube. This was my first real step into more complex 2-layer routing and designing for someone else’s requirements.

---

### 4. Early–Mid Boards (Lost Projects)  
Folder: [`lost-boards`](./lost-boards)  

Several boards created between my earliest designs and my more recent work were permanently lost during a Windows corruption incident. This folder documents what those boards represented and the lessons they taught me, even though no files or screenshots remain.
